Skyline High School
Alumni Soccer Games

Background

The day after Thanksgiving each year, alumni from the boys soccer team at Skyline High School (map) in Oakland, California meet to play a soccer game. Traditionally the alumni played against the current varsity team, but the varsity team has not attended recently, so nowadays the alumni scrimmage amongst themselves. These games have been played since 1973:

The alumni games officially started on Friday, November 23, 1973, which was my first alumni game, having graduated in June of that year. As I recall, a number of my graduated mates came back to play, including Gary Frazier, Mike Conley, Brian Sinnott, Omar Suer and a few others that I can't remember (that was a long time ago!). I believe we had to play with several of the varsity players out of a group that included Don Craig, Herb Schmidt, Eric Rudney, et. al.

Leland Heiner
Class of 1973

The games were held at Merritt College (map) near Skyline every year since I became involved (starting in 1979), but in 2006 we decided to try a different location because we had been kicked off Merritt in recent years by campus security for not having a paid permit, which would cost at least $180.

The 2010 Game #2

Thanks to tireless organizing efforts from Patrick Riley, the current varsity team was allowed to scrimmage against the alumni at 3:30pm on Friday, December 10, 2010 on Skyline's field.

Skyline's football/soccer field was converted from grass into FieldTurf in 2003. This was the first time I had seen the new field. Although I generally think grass is better than artificial surfaces, I like Skyline's new field because the old grass field was usually muddy and full of pot holes.

The weather was overcast and heavy fog and mist descended onto the field during the game, but this cool weather was perfect for playing.

The alumni didn't find out about the game until the night before, so it was difficult for people to rearrange their schedules to play. We scrounged together 11 players to begin the game, including 3 of the coach's children. More alumni arrived as time went on. I think we ended up with 13 players and 2 observers, but I don't think all of the players on the alumni team were alumni.

The game was scoreless for about 20 minutes, then the varsity team scored about 4 goals before halftime. The coach asked us to use all of our players in the second half, to put more pressure on the varsity team. Playing numbers up helped the alumni. The varsity team didn't score many goals in the second half, and the alumni had some good scoring chances but didn't finish any.

Although the varsity team won about 5-0, I didn't feel embarrassed to be playing against them because I thought we provided a reasonable challenge for them. I was impressed by the ball skills and speed of several of the varsity players, and overall the varsity team played well together and performed some nice combination play, but several of the alumni players appeared capable of matching up well against the varsity players.

After the game, the coach appeared pleased because it allowed him to evaluate players in a realistic game setting before they begin their league season. He expressed interest in continuing this resurrected connection with the alumni, so hopefully we will be able to schedule another event with the varsity team next year.
